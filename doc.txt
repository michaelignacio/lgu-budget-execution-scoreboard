II. Background and Organizational Context

You are invited to join a team of dedicated development professionals whose primary role is to support the Philippines to achieve the 2030 Agenda. As part of the UNDP team your focus will be to work with multiple stakeholders to find country specific solutions that lead to the eradication of poverty and to ensure no one is left behind.

You will be confronted with diverse development challenges, including addressing basic development needs, supporting institutional transformations, and strengthening development pathways in contexts of crisis and recovery. These challenges require integrated and systems-oriented solutions.

UNDP Philippines, through its Institutions and Partnerships portfolio, advances reforms in public financial management, procurement, anti-corruption, justice sector strengthening, and digital governance.

Public finance and digital governance are central to UNDP’s governance agenda, focusing on strengthening the capacity of government institutions to plan, allocate, execute, and account for public resources effectively, while leveraging digital technologies to enhance transparency, efficiency, and accountability. This includes budget formulation and execution, treasury systems, fiscal transparency, and the digitalization of public finance systems such as IFMIS, e-procurement, and data platforms across national and subnational levels, including BARMM.

The Country Office is strengthening its engagement in accountable public finance and GovTech, promoting data-driven governance, interoperability of systems, and digital solutions that improve public service delivery and institutional integrity.

We place a premium on national ownership and partnership-based approaches. In the public finance and digital governance domain, UNDP works closely with central fiscal agencies, digital governance counterparts, local government units, and development partners to strengthen fiscal systems, modernize digital infrastructure, and improve public sector performance.

UNDP seeks individuals who can apply global best practices in public finance and digital governance to national contexts. You will have strong knowledge and demonstrated experience in governance, particularly in public financial management and digital governance systems, with exposure to governance reforms in the Philippines.

Your role will contribute to building and managing a portfolio of activities that strengthen public finance systems, improve budget execution, and support the digital transformation of governance systems, positioning UNDP as a strategic partner in fiscal reform, GovTech, and government financing.
You will work collaboratively with colleagues across governance pillars, UN agencies, government institutions, donors, and civil society.




III. Position Purpose

Under the guidance of the Outcome Lead (Institutions and Partnerships), the Programme Analyst – Public Finance and Digital Governance will lead the day-to-day management of a portfolio of activities contributing to fiscal systems strengthening, digital transformation, and public sector effectiveness.

The Programme Analyst will play a key role in advancing integrated public finance and digital governance reforms by supporting initiatives that improve budget execution, strengthen fiscal systems, and leverage digital technologies to enhance transparency, efficiency, and accountability.

The Programme Analyst will:
Support initiatives on public financial management, budget execution, and fiscal systems;
Support the design and implementation of digital governance solutions related to public finance, including IFMIS, e-procurement, fiscal data systems, and interoperability platforms;
Contribute to strengthening institutional linkages between planning, budgeting, procurement, and service delivery;
Support policy dialogue and institutional reforms related to public finance and digital governance;
Support monitoring, evaluation, knowledge management, and pipeline development;
Contribute to strengthening end-to-end accountability by linking digital public finance systems with procurement, anti-corruption, and justice sector accountability mechanisms.

The Programme Analyst will supervise a Programme Associate and Assistant and support Project Managers, contributing to cross-practice coordination and portfolio integration.
